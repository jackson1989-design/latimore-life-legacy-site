The Life-Changing Event

Recovering at Pocono Medical Center, December 2010

On December 7, 2010, during a college basketball game at East Stroudsburg University, Jackson went into sudden cardiac arrest. His heart stopped on the court. He was 22 years old.

He survived because athletic trainers Wendy Dietrich and Colleen Shotwell, along with EMT Douglas Shaffer, had immediate access to an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) and knew how to use it. They shocked his heart back to life and administered CPR until emergency services arrived.

Local news coverage of the life-saving event - Pocono Record, December 2010

That AED was placed at ESU through the advocacy of the Gregory W. Moyer Defibrillator Fund - a nonprofit created after 15-year-old Greg Moyer died of sudden cardiac arrest during a basketball game in 2000. Greg's family's mission to place AEDs in schools and public buildings across Pennsylvania directly saved Jackson's life a decade later.

"That experience taught me that preparedness is everything. The Moyer family's advocacy prepared that gym to save my life. Now I help families prepare financially for the unexpected, because I know firsthand that tomorrow is never guaranteed."

Mission & Values

Latimore Life & Legacy was founded in November 2025 with a clear mission: break generational cycles of financial vulnerability in post-industrial Pennsylvania communities.

Jackson serves working families, pre-retirees, and local organizations who need honest guidance, not high-pressure sales tactics. As an independent contractor, he's free to recommend the best solutions from multiple carriers - putting client needs first, always.

His approach combines academic rigor (MPA, MBA) with real-world empathy earned through direct service work. He understands policy analysis AND the human impact of financial insecurity.

Community Involvement

Jackson is deeply connected to the Pennsylvania Anthracite Coal Region - an area facing unique economic challenges after decades of industrial decline. He believes that financial literacy and proper protection strategies can help break cycles of poverty and instability.

Through youth coaching, he teaches discipline, goal-setting, and the importance of planning ahead - lessons that apply equally to sports and financial security.
