Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from a 401(k) or IRA?

401(k)s and IRAs are tax-deferred, meaning you pay taxes later when you withdraw. Tax-advantaged strategies using indexed universal life or Roth conversions create tax-free income that never gets taxed.

What does "0% floor" mean?

With indexed strategies, your account is linked to market indexes (like the S&P 500) for growth, but if the market crashes, you lose nothing. Your floor is 0% — you can't go negative.

Is there a minimum to get started?

Most tax-advantaged wealth strategies work best with contributions of $300-$2,000/month, but we can design plans for any budget depending on your goals.

Can I access my money before retirement?

Yes! Unlike 401(k)s that penalize early withdrawals, properly structured indexed universal life policies allow tax-free loans at any age with no penalties.

What if the 2026 tax laws change (TCJA Sunset)?

That's precisely why we're acting now. The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act expires in 2026, potentially raising tax rates by 3-5%. Locking in tax-advantaged strategies today protects you from future increases.

Is this guaranteed or an investment?

These are insurance-based strategies, not investments. They offer contractual guarantees with upside potential based on index performance, but are not securities or stocks.
