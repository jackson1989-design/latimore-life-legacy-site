Frequently Asked Questions

Why not just use a 529 plan?

529 plans reduce financial aid eligibility and can only be used for education. Life insurance cash value isn't counted on FAFSA and can be used for anything tax-free.

What if my child doesn't go to college?

The cash value can be used for a business startup, down payment on a home, or kept as your own retirement fund. It's completely flexible.

How much should I contribute?

Most families contribute $200-$1,000/month depending on goals. A $500/month contribution for 18 years can build $150,000-$200,000 in accessible cash value.

Is the policy on me or my child?

The policy is on the parent to maximize cash value. Policies on children have limited contribution capacity and slower growth.

What happens to the death benefit?

While funding education, the policy also provides life insurance protection. If something happens to you, the death benefit pays off the home and provides for your family.

When should I start?

The younger your child, the better. Starting when they're 0-5 years old gives you 13-18 years of tax-free compounding growth.
