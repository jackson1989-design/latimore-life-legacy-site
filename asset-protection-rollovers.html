Frequently Asked Questions

Will I pay taxes or penalties on a rollover?

No. Direct rollovers from 401(k), 403(b), or pension plans to IRAs or annuities are tax-free and penalty-free as long as the money moves directly between custodians.

What's the difference between an IRA and an annuity rollover?

Both preserve tax-deferred status. An IRA offers investment flexibility but market risk. An annuity offers guaranteed income, downside protection, and living benefits for long-term care.

How long does a rollover take?

Most rollovers complete in 7-21 business days depending on your old plan administrator's processing time.

Can I roll over while still employed?

Usually no, unless you're over 59½ and your plan allows in-service distributions. Most rollovers happen after you leave an employer.

What if I have multiple old 401(k) accounts?

You can consolidate all of them into a single rollover IRA or annuity, simplifying management and reducing fees.

Is my money locked up in an annuity?

Most fixed indexed annuities allow 10% penalty-free withdrawals annually. After the surrender period (typically 7-10 years), you have full access.
